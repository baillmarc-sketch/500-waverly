# 500 Waverly Ave · PH4 — Render & Design Brief
### Direction: *Warm, Edited, Modern — the gallery dinner-party apartment*

A working brief for generating a handful of 3D / photoreal renders of the unit, written
from three lenses — **architect**, **interior designer**, and **render artist**. It pairs
the mood board with what the apartment actually is (oak floors, exposed beams, navy & blush
accent walls, a walnut U-kitchen with a white-quartz waterfall peninsula, wraparound corner
glass, roof terrace). Hand this to a 3D artist, or paste the per-room prompts into an
AI image tool (Flux / Midjourney v6 / DALL·E 3 / Gemini "Nano-banana" / Sora image).

---

## 1. The direction in one paragraph
Warm, collected, and unfussy — "less decorated, more curated." Low-slung oatmeal bouclé
seating, walnut and oak wood tones, brushed steel and aged brass metals, canvas and linen,
stone. A gallery sensibility (art as the room's main event, generous negative space) crossed
with a dinner-party warmth (round tables, candlelight, a bar moment, music). Nothing precious;
everything touchable. Modern with soul.

## 2. Palette & materials (from the board → mapped to the unit)
**Neutrals (90%):** cream · warm white · taupe · oatmeal · greige.
**Warm woods:** walnut (furniture) + the existing white-oak floors and walnut kitchen.
**Anchors (deep tones):** charcoal, tobacco, black.
**Accents (use sparingly, ~10%):** deep red, cobalt, mustard, forest green, burnt orange.
**Metals:** brushed steel + aged brass (mix warm/cool, don't over-match).
**Materials:** canvas/linen, bouclé, travertine/limestone, leather (tobacco), ceramic, raw wood.

> **The palette tension to resolve:** the unit's architecture is currently **navy + blush**;
> the board is **earthy/warm**. Two ways to land it (we'll render both):
> - **A · Keep navy + blush** — treat navy as a moody gallery anchor and *flood it with warmth*
>   (oak, oatmeal bouclé, tobacco leather, brass, big art). Blush already reads warm and plays
>   beautifully with tobacco + walnut. Zero painting; renter-friendly.
> - **B · Warm the walls** — repaint navy → a warm deep tone (**bronze-olive or warm charcoal**),
>   keep or soften blush toward **plaster/clay**. Fully on-board, more committed.

## 3. Designer's furniture plan (ties to the planner layouts)
- **Living:** low oatmeal **bouclé sectional or sofa + sculptural lounge chair** (the curvy
  shearling chair on the board), round **travertine/wood coffee table**, dark-wood low **media
  console** under the wall-mounted TV. Floor lamp + a mushroom table lamp for layered light.
- **Dining:** round **walnut pedestal table** (seats 4–6), **mixed modern chairs** (a couple of
  black/cane, a couple upholstered) — the "collected" look. Low bowl + taper candles.
- **Kitchen:** it's already walnut + white quartz — style only. Brass hardware accents, a
  single piece of art leaned on the counter, one olive/ceramic moment, warm under-cabinet light.
- **Bedroom:** **walnut platform bed** (command position, east wall), dark-wood nightstands,
  two **mushroom lamps**, linen bedding in cream/taupe with one tobacco or forest accent, a
  large piece of art over the bed, low dresser under the corner window.
- **Art & objects:** gallery hang (one large + small clusters), ceramic vases, stacked art
  books, a worn rug with a faded red/rust note (per the board's textile + carpet swatches).

## 4. Render-artist notes (apply to every prompt)
- **Camera:** full-frame, **24–35mm** for rooms (no fisheye), eye level ~150cm, two-point
  perspective (verticals kept vertical). Shallow-ish DoF only for detail shots.
- **Light:** large-window daylight, **golden-hour or blue-hour** for the dinner-party mood;
  mix in warm practicals (lamps, candles, under-cabinet). Soft shadows, no harsh flash.
- **Style keywords:** *photorealistic interior architectural photography, editorial, natural
  materials, soft volumetric daylight, film-like color, Kinfolk / Cereal magazine aesthetic.*
- **Negative / avoid:** *clutter, plastic, glossy CGI look, oversaturation, fisheye, warped
  lines, text, watermark, people, HDR halos, cool fluorescent light.*
- **Consistency:** keep oak floors, white ceiling with **exposed beams**, and black-framed
  corner glass across all living/dining shots so the set reads as one apartment.

## 6. How to actually generate these
1. **Fastest (today):** paste each prompt into **Flux**, **Midjourney v6**, **DALL·E 3**,
   **Gemini image (Nano-banana)**, or **Sora image**. Generate A and B per room, pick favorites,
   iterate by editing one variable at a time (light → palette → furniture).
2. **Most accurate (dimensional):** give this brief + the planner floor plan to a **3D artist**
   (SketchUp + V-Ray, Blender + Cycles, or D5/Twinmotion). The room sizes and layouts are in
   `data.js`; the shot list is §5.
3. **Drive it from here:** connect an **image-generation MCP server** to this Claude Code
   environment; then I can run these prompts, compare A/B, and iterate the set directly.
